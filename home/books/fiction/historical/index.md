# :material-timer-sand:{ .anim-rotate } Historical Fiction

History reimagined through narrative, blending fact and speculation to bring the past to life.

### Fatherland, by Robert Harris (2025)
A unique blend of history and speculation. This time the author imagines an alternate history where Germany won the Second World War.

### Enigma, by Robert Harris (2025)
Set in Bletchley Park during World War II. The protagonist is a code-breaker. The story itself unfolds like an enigma.

### Second Sleep, by Robert Harris (2025)
A priest goes to a remote town after his predecessor passes away, and has to solve the mystery of his death.

### ❤️Lustrum & Dictator, by Robert Harris (2025)
Covers the life of Cicero as he watches the rise of Caesar. Fascinating to see the story from the 'other' side. Highly recommended.

### I, Claudius, by Robert Graves (2025)
A sprawling history of several Roman emperors from Augustus to Tiberius and Caligula, ending with the author himself.

### The Glass Palace, by Amitav Ghosh (2024)
Amitav Ghosh is at his best when he is writing historical fiction like this. A sprawling multi-generational story across Burma and India.

### The Double Tongue, by William Golding (2024)
The story of an oracle and her cynical priest, set in Delphi during Greece's twilight years.

### The Iron King, by Maurice Druon (2024)
An excellent historical novel based on King Philip the Fair.

### The Miniaturist, by Kunal Basu (2024)
About a painter in Mughal India. A pleasant evening's read.

### Circe & The Song of Achilles, by Madeline Miller (2020)
Lovely books from the author who brings Greek mythology to life with a lyrical touch.

### Mythos, by Stephen Fry (2020)
Has his trademark light-hearted air which is really nice sometimes and a bit annoying some other times.
